About Me

Biograpghy

Omar Tanner

  I'm a self-driven, somewhat minimalistic guy, pursuing whatever interests me. Space, art, cats: these words instantly come to mind when I think of awesome things, for different (potentially related) reasons. I'm still trying to find what exactly it is that makes something appeal to me.
  One thing I stand by is to never compare yourself to others - let them be themselves, you be yourself, then nobody gets hurt. One's life is far too valuable to follow another's footsteps. To me there is a fine line between inspiration and replication. I try not to cross that line, which often means distancing myself from those nearing it. Anyone who can embrace themselves, while pertaining the ability to inspire from others, is someone whom I'd love to meet. That, however, is not neccessarily a description of me.

  Computers have fascinated me from a young age, precisely from the era of Nintendo DS's; being (healthily) addicted to Super Mario Bros and similar games. In school and at home I'd do whatever I could to access a computer, so I'd say my future in Computer Science totally stemmed from a fascination of computers; aided by my family's restrictions on them in my childhood, making my interactions with them nothing else than a treat. I also enjoyed toying around with electronics, making make-shift torches, and occasionally (to the annoyance of my parents) taking apart household electronic devices.
  In my free time, I apply the knowledge gained through my studies practically, building plugins and tools for the games I play. The mix of problem-solving, creativity and hard work pays of a lot for me, as once finishing a project I feel a great sense of completion and contribution. Therefore, I intend to pursue similar challenges into the future; designing and implementing software to solve complex problems, for the greater good of humanity.

  As important as hard work is to me, down-time is equally as important, for without it I'd burn out (as has happened before). Work-life balance is extremely important to me, as in my eyes one's down-time goes hand-in-hand with their productivity to a certain extent, as paradoxical as it may be. To relieve stress, working out has been extremely effective for me over the past few years, and to relax I enjoy watching films, listening to music, and generally being outdoors.
